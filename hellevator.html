Hellevator

Grade:

9.0

Study Period:

Third-semester project in BSc

Duration:

21 days

Technologies:

Unity, C#, Blender, Krita, Mixamo

Team Size:

4

Try the game on Itch.io

Hellevator is a top-down rogue-like game with a comedic twist, marking our first-ever game development project. Players assume the role of Jesus Christ on a mission to save his friend Greg, who has been unfairly dragged to hell by Satan. Throughout the game, players must navigate increasingly challenging floors swarming with various demonic enemies while utilising a sword, shuriken, and holy dash ability to survive. As players progress, they can level up and purchase upgrades using the souls of defeated enemies.

My Contributions:

  • Developed ranged mechanics for player, turret, and ranged enemy
  • Implemented AI for enemies, including predictive movement and collision avoidance
  • Designed visual elements, including ranged enemy models, textures, and effects
  • Programmed health and dash systems
  • Added falling debris mechanic
  • Created the starting scene
  • Contributed to bug fixes and overall game polish

Lessons Learned:

  • The value of learning from others' experiences and failures in game development
  • Playtest early and playtest a lot
  • Importance of a code freeze day for final polishing and game balancing
  • Recognising that tasks can take significantly longer than anticipated
  • Ensuring game balancing is tuned for players, not just developers

Teacher Feedback

Excellent work! Your steady work and great attitude resulted in an interesting game with a very solid core gameplay loop. You are missing a bit out on achievable objectives. I understand you wanted to make a challenging game but in the context of displaying it on Demo Day it would have been appropriate to balance the curve out a bit more. It's a bit unclear still when and how Jesus takes damage. Having health only being regenerated in shops meant that the cool upgrades were behind an unachievable paywall. Shorter levels, the store showing up more often and souls being more plentiful would have fixed most things. There's no good reason to not let people get your upgrades, especially in a context like demo day. The game felt good to play and your context and theme really helped out the gameplay in a cohesive way. Congrats!
